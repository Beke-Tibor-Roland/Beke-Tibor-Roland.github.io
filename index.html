Data Breach Intelligence

Analyzing cybersecurity incidents, breach methods, and digital vulnerabilities

Cybersecurity Intelligence

This cybersecurity intelligence dashboard analyzes data breach incidents across industries and timeframes. Through interactive visualizations, we examine breach methods, exposed record volumes, and security vulnerabilities that impact organizations worldwide. Our analysis reveals critical patterns in cyber threats, helping security professionals, organizations, and policymakers understand the evolving landscape of digital security. Each visualization uncovers insights about attack vectors, vulnerable sectors, and the scale of data exposure in our increasingly connected world.

Platform Breach Analysis

Data Breaches by Social Media Platform

Analysis

This visualization reveals the staggering scale of data breaches across major social media platforms, exposing millions of user records to potential misuse. Each bar represents the volume of compromised user data, highlighting how vulnerable our digital identities have become. Facebook's breach of over 858 million records in 2019 stands as a stark reminder of the consequences of inadequate security measures. These incidents demonstrate that even the largest tech companies struggle with protecting user data, underscoring the urgent need for enhanced security protocols and transparency in how personal information is safeguarded.

Breach Timeline

Data Breach Records Trend Over Time

Analysis

This timeline reveals an alarming escalation of data breach incidents over nearly two decades, with exposed records growing from millions to billions. The dramatic peaks in 2013 (3.47 billion), 2018 (1.53 billion), and 2019 (3.82 billion records) mark watershed moments in cybersecurity history. The upward trajectory demonstrates that as our digital footprint expands, so does our vulnerability. Despite increased awareness and security investments, the scale of breaches continues to grow, suggesting that existing security frameworks may be insufficient against evolving attack methods. This trend demands a fundamental rethinking of how we protect digital assets in an interconnected world.

Attack Vector Analysis

Breach Methods vs Number of Incidents

Analysis

This scatter plot analyzes breach method categories and their frequency of incidents. Each point represents a categorized breach method, positioned by category (x-axis) and number of incidents (y-axis). The methods have been grouped into broader categories such as External Attack, Poor Security/Configuration, Accidental Exposure, Physical Loss/Theft, Insider Threat, and others. The size of each circle indicates the total volume of records exposed for that category, while the color intensity reflects the frequency of incidents. The visualization reveals which breach categories occur most frequently and helps identify the most common attack vectors. By categorizing methods, we can better understand the primary security failure modes and focus prevention efforts on the most prevalent threat categories.
